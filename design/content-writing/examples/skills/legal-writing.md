# Legal Writing Examples

## Example 1 — Confidentiality clause
**Prompt:** Draft a simple confidentiality clause.

**Output:**
**Confidentiality.** Each Party shall keep confidential and shall not disclose to any third party any non-public business, technical, financial, or commercial information received from the other Party in connection with this Agreement (“Confidential Information”), except to its employees, contractors, or professional advisers who have a need to know such information and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set out herein.

## Why it works
- Defined term is introduced.
- Obligation and exception are visible.
- The sentence performs a clear legal function.

## Example 2 — Plain-language legal summary
**Prompt:** Summarize a non-compete clause in plain English.

**Output:**
This clause says the employee cannot work for a directly competing business or start a competing business for a limited period after leaving the company. Whether that restriction is enforceable depends heavily on local law, including the scope, duration, geography, and the employee’s role.

## Why it works
- Legal effect is translated clearly.
- Jurisdiction sensitivity is flagged.
- The summary does not overclaim.

---

## Example 3 — Notice provision
**Prompt:** Draft a notice clause.

**Output:**
**Notices.** Any notice required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed duly given when delivered by hand, sent by recognized courier, or transmitted by email to the contact details designated by the receiving Party, provided that the sender retains reasonable evidence of transmission or delivery.

## Why it works
- Mechanism is specified.
- Trigger for deemed delivery is clear.
- Proof standard is included.

---

## Example 4 — Red flag note
**Prompt:** Identify a problem in this clause: “The Company may terminate the contract whenever it wants.”

**Output:**
Red flag: the clause is broad, informal, and likely to create dispute over timing, grounds, notice, and consequences. It should specify whether termination is for convenience, for cause, upon what notice period, and what happens to accrued rights and payment obligations.

## Why it works
- It spots ambiguity.
- It converts criticism into drafting guidance.
