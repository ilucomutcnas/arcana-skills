# Copy Editing — Examples

## Seven Sweeps Quick Reference

| Sweep | Focus | Key Question |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1. Clarity | Understanding | Can the reader understand immediately? |
| 2. Voice | Consistency | Does it sound the same throughout? |
| 3. So What | Relevance | Does every claim earn its place? |
| 4. Prove It | Evidence | Is every claim substantiated? |
| 5. Specificity | Concreteness | Is it concrete enough to compel? |
| 6. Emotion | Feeling | Does it make the reader feel something? |
| 7. Zero Risk | Barriers | Have we removed every barrier to action? |

## Common Problems and Fixes

- **Wall of Features**: add "which means..." after each feature to bridge to benefits
- **Corporate Speak**: ask "How would a human say this?" and use those words
- **Weak Opening**: lead with the reader's problem or desired outcome
- **Buried CTA**: make the ask obvious, early, and repeated
- **No Proof**: add specific testimonials, numbers, or case references
- **Generic Claims**: specify who, how, and by how much
- **Mixed Audiences**: pick one audience and write directly to them

## Collaborative Editing Flow

1. Run a sweep and present findings
2. Recommend specific edits (not just problems)
3. Request the updated copy from the author
4. Verify previous sweeps after each round
5. Repeat until a full sweep finds no new issues
