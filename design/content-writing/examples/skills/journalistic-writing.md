# Journalistic Writing Examples

## Example 1 — Straight news lede
**Prompt:** Write a short news-style opening about a city launching electric buses.

**Output:**
The city of Bristol said Monday that it will add 120 electric buses to its public transport fleet by early next year, a move officials say is intended to cut emissions and reduce fuel costs across the network.

A city transport report says the rollout will begin in the autumn, with the first vehicles assigned to routes serving the center and several outer districts. It remains unclear whether fares will change as part of the transition.

## Why it works
- The core development arrives immediately.
- Claims are attributed.
- The second paragraph adds context and uncertainty.

## Example 2 — Feature opening
**Prompt:** Write a feature-style opening about farmers adapting to drought.

**Output:**
By sunrise, the soil in Miguel Herrera's field already carries the look of late afternoon: pale, cracked, reluctant to hold what little moisture the night leaves behind. For growers across the region, drought is no longer the bad season. It is the baseline.

## Why it works
- It begins with a concrete image.
- It widens quickly into the larger point.
- The tone is controlled rather than melodramatic.

---

## Example 3 — Explainer passage
**Prompt:** Explain what a central bank digital currency is for a general audience.

**Output:**
A central bank digital currency, or CBDC, is a digital form of state-issued money. Unlike cryptocurrency systems that are typically run on decentralized networks, a CBDC would be issued and backed by a country's central bank. Supporters say it could modernize payments. Critics warn that design choices around privacy and control will matter as much as the technology itself.

## Why it works
- It defines the term directly.
- It contrasts the concept with something readers may know.
- It includes competing perspectives without false balance.

---

## Example 4 — Risky claim handling
**Prompt:** Rewrite this sentence in a more journalistic way: “The company lied to users for years.”

**Output:**
Users and consumer advocates have accused the company of misleading customers for years, though the company disputes that characterization.

## Why it works
- It converts accusation into attributed language.
- It adds the contested nature of the claim.
- It removes unsupported certainty.

---

## Example 5 — Headline set
**Prompt:** Give 5 newsroom-style headlines for a report about hospitals using AI triage systems.

**Output:**
1. Hospitals Expand Use of AI Triage Tools as Patient Volumes Rise
2. AI Triage Systems Spread Through Hospitals, Raising Oversight Questions
3. More Hospitals Turn to AI for Intake Decisions
4. Health Systems Test AI Triage Tools Under Staffing Pressure
5. Hospitals Adopt AI Screening Tools While Regulators Watch Closely

## Why it works
- The lines are informative, not gimmicky.
- Each headline contains a clear subject and action.
- The language stays publication-ready.
