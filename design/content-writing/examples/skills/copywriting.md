# Copywriting — Examples

## Copy Brief Summary Example

```
- Page goal: Convert free trial signups on the landing page
- Target audience: Marketing managers at mid-size B2B companies
- Core value proposition: Automated reporting saves 10+ hours/week
- Primary CTA: Start free trial
- Traffic context: Paid search (solution-aware)
- Assumptions: Audience has tried manual reporting, familiar with competitors
```

## Above-the-Fold Example

**Headline:** Stop Building Reports by Hand

**Subheadline:** Connect your data sources once and get automated, branded reports delivered to your inbox every week. No code required.

**Primary CTA:** Start your free trial

## Section Structure Example

```
1. Hero: headline, subheadline, CTA
2. Social proof: "Trusted by 2,847 marketing teams"
3. Problem: "Manual reporting wastes 10+ hours every week"
4. Solution: 3 key benefits with outcome language
5. How it works: 3 steps (connect, configure, receive)
6. Objection handling: FAQ (pricing, security, integrations)
7. Final CTA: "Start free — no credit card required"
```

## Headline Alternatives with Rationale

1. "Stop Building Reports by Hand" — direct pain point, immediate relevance
2. "Your Weekly Reports, Done in 5 Minutes" — outcome-focused, time contrast
3. "Automated Reports for Marketing Teams" — audience-specific, clear offer

## Claim Discipline

- Good: "2,847 teams use automated reporting" — specific, verifiable
- Bad: "Thousands of companies trust us" — vague, unverifiable
- Good: "Reports in 5 minutes" — specific outcome
- Bad: "Lightning-fast reporting" — buzzword, no substance
