# Language Handling Examples

## Example 1 — Mixed-language cleanup
**Prompt:** Rewrite this into clean English: “We need подписать договор before Friday because клиент already asked twice.”

**Output:**
We need to sign the contract before Friday because the client has already asked twice.

## Why it works
- Mixed-language fragments are normalized.
- Meaning is preserved.
- The final sentence is clean and consistent.

---

## Example 2 — Bilingual business output
**Prompt:** Write a short partnership invitation in English and Azerbaijani.

**Output:**
**English**  
We would be glad to discuss a potential partnership and explore formats that could be useful for both sides.

**Azerbaijani**  
Potensial tərəfdaşlığı müzakirə etmək və hər iki tərəf üçün faydalı ola biləcək formatları nəzərdən keçirməkdən məmnun olarıq.

## Why it works
- Parallel structure is maintained.
- Each language reads naturally.
- The tone remains professional in both versions.

---

## Example 3 — Marketing localization
**Prompt:** Translate “Own your privacy” into Russian for product marketing.

**Output:**
Возьмите приватность под свой контроль.

## Why it works
- It localizes the idea rather than translating word-for-word.
- The result sounds like real marketing copy.
- The imperative stays natural.

---

## Example 4 — Legal term preservation
**Prompt:** Translate a legal paragraph into English but preserve the term “Eelisosad”.

**Output:**
The holders of Eelisosad shall have the preferential rights set out in this Agreement, including dividend priority and the liquidation preferences expressly described below.

## Why it works
- The defined term stays unchanged.
- The surrounding clause reads naturally in English.
- Legal function is preserved.
