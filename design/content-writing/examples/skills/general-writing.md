# General Writing Examples

## Example 1 — Friendly birthday message
**Prompt:** Write a warm but not cheesy birthday message for a friend.

**Output:**
Happy birthday, my friend. I’m really glad you’re in my life. I hope this year brings you more peace, more wins, and at least a few moments where everything feels exactly right. You deserve a good day.

## Why it works
- Warm without becoming exaggerated.
- Specific emotional register.
- Clean, human ending.

---

## Example 2 — Gentle apology
**Prompt:** Write a simple apology text.

**Output:**
I’ve been thinking about how I handled that, and I know I made it harder than it needed to be. I’m sorry. You didn’t deserve that from me.

## Why it works
- Direct accountability.
- No excuses.
- Emotionally honest and concise.

---

## Example 3 — Casual invitation
**Prompt:** Invite friends over this weekend.

**Output:**
Thinking of having a small get-together on Saturday evening if you’re free. Nothing formal — just food, music, and a chance to catch up. Would be great to see you.

## Why it works
- Natural and easygoing.
- Clear without sounding scripted.
- Appropriate for everyday use.

---

## Example 4 — Social caption
**Prompt:** Write a simple caption for a rainy city photo.

**Output:**
Rainy streets, slow traffic, good coffee. Not the worst kind of evening.

## Why it works
- Minimal and natural.
- Light mood without trying too hard.
