# Business Writing Examples

## Example 1 — Meeting follow-up
**Prompt:** Write a short email after a product meeting.

**Output:**
Subject: Follow-up on today’s product review

Hi team,

Thanks for the discussion today. To confirm the outcome, we agreed on three next steps:

1. Finalize the onboarding flow by Thursday.
2. Send revised pricing language for review by Friday noon.
3. Prepare a short demo version for partner testing next week.

The main open issue is whether we keep the current signup screen or simplify it before launch.

Please reply in-thread if any of the action items need correction.

Best,  
Nina

## Why it works
- Outcome is clear.
- Action items are concrete.
- The open issue is isolated.

## Example 2 — Partnership outreach
**Prompt:** Write a short partner email.

**Output:**
Subject: Potential collaboration between our teams

Hello Mr. Rahman,

I’m reaching out from BrightNorth Media. We believe there may be a strong fit between your organization’s upcoming education initiatives and our audience.

We would welcome a short meeting to discuss possible formats, including sponsored coverage, branded content, and event media partnership.

If this is relevant, I’d be glad to send a short deck in advance and suggest a few time slots.

Kind regards,  
Sara Ahmed

## Why it works
- Purpose is clear.
- Tone is respectful and commercially appropriate.
- The next step is easy.

---

## Example 3 — Internal chat
**Prompt:** Write a Slack message asking for a decision.

**Output:**
Need a final yes/no on the revised homepage copy today so design can lock the build. Current draft is in the doc. Main change is the new CTA language. Please comment by 4 p.m. if you want edits before sign-off.

## Why it works
- Fast, direct, actionable.
- Time sensitivity is explicit.
- No wasted words.
