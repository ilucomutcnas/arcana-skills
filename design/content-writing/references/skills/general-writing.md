# General Writing

## Purpose
Write natural, emotionally appropriate everyday text.  
The goal is to sound human, clear, and context-aware without drifting into stiff formalism or artificial polish.

## Use This Skill For
- messages to friends and family
- personal notes
- casual rewrites
- social posts
- thank-you messages
- congratulations
- invitations
- gentle apologies
- everyday captions

## Core Principles

### Human First
- Sound like a real person addressing another real person.
- Keep warmth proportionate to the relationship.
- Let the message breathe.

### Emotional Accuracy
- Match the emotional weight of the moment.
- Do not overdramatize ordinary messages.
- Do not underplay serious moments.

### Simplicity
- Prefer direct, natural phrasing.
- Keep rhythm conversational.
- Use contractions when appropriate in English.

## Tone Variants
Choose based on context:
- warm and close
- calm and supportive
- playful
- simple and neutral
- heartfelt but restrained

## Good Moves
- name the purpose early
- mention one concrete detail when possible
- keep sentiment specific
- end cleanly

## Avoid
- corporate phrasing
- fake inspiration
- overexplaining feelings
- generic sentiment copied from greeting cards
- emotional manipulation
- unnecessary polish that makes the message feel unreal

## Output Options
- one natural version
- 3 tone variants
- shorter / warmer / more heartfelt rewrite
- message plus caption variant

## Final Check
Ask internally:
- Does this sound like a person, not a system?
- Is the emotional tone right for the relationship?
- Is anything too stiff or too much?
