# Legal Writing

## Purpose
Write in a legally structured, risk-aware manner.  
The goal is precision, consistency, and controlled language — not stylistic flair.

## Use This Skill For
- contract clauses
- templates
- notices
- disclaimers
- policies
- internal legal summaries
- issue-spotting rewrites
- formal definitions
- structured obligations and rights

## Core Rule
Do not present draft language as jurisdiction-proof legal advice.  
When the legal effect depends on jurisdiction, regulation, enforceability, licensing, tax treatment, consumer law, employment law, securities law, or litigation risk, signal that review by qualified counsel is required.

## Drafting Standards

### Precision
- Define important terms.
- Use consistent labels for parties, dates, obligations, and documents.
- Avoid fuzzy verbs when legal effect matters.

### Logical Structure
- Separate:
  - definitions
  - rights
  - obligations
  - limitations
  - remedies
  - termination
  - governing mechanics
- Keep conditions and exceptions visible.

### Risk Awareness
- Identify ambiguity.
- Flag missing variables.
- Avoid accidental overpromising.
- Avoid broad statements that create obligations the drafter may not want.

## Preferred Language Patterns
Use:
- “For the purposes of this Agreement…”
- “Subject to…”
- “Except as otherwise provided…”
- “The Party shall…”
- “No Party shall…”
- “Upon written notice…”
- “To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law…”

Use **shall**, **may**, **must**, **will**, **is entitled to**, and **is not obligated to** deliberately.

## Avoid
- conversational phrasing
- undefined key terms
- rhetorical or emotional language
- mixed obligation verbs without reason
- vague timing such as “soon” or “promptly” unless defined or acceptable
- promises that belong in commercial copy rather than legal text

## Useful Output Modes
- plain-language legal summary
- clause draft
- clause rewrite for clarity
- issue list
- policy section
- template skeleton with placeholders
- red flag memo

## Final Check
Ask internally:
- Are all operative terms consistent?
- Does each sentence have one legal job?
- Are exceptions and conditions visible?
- Is anything being implied that should be explicit?
- Does this need a jurisdiction caveat?
