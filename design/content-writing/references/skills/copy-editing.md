## Purpose

Systematically improve existing copy through focused editing passes. Good copy editing is not about rewriting — it is about enhancing. Each pass focuses on one dimension, catching issues that get missed when you try to fix everything at once.

Key principles: do not change the core message, multiple focused passes beat one unfocused review, each edit should have a clear reason, preserve the author's voice while improving clarity.

## When to Use

- When reviewing and improving existing marketing or conversion copy
- When editing a first draft produced by copywriting
- When doing a focused quality pass on any user-facing text
- When running a structured review before publication

## Workflow — The Seven Sweeps Framework

Edit through seven sequential passes, each focusing on one dimension. After each sweep, loop back to check previous sweeps are not compromised.

### Sweep 1: Clarity
Can the reader understand what you are saying? Check for confusing structures, unclear pronoun references, jargon, ambiguous statements, missing context. After this sweep, confirm the Rule of One (one idea per section) and You Rule (copy speaks to the reader).

### Sweep 2: Voice and Tone
Is the copy consistent in how it sounds? Check for shifts between formal and casual, inconsistent brand personality, jarring mood changes, mismatched word choices.

### Sweep 3: So What
Does every claim earn its place? Check features missing benefits, impressive-but-empty statements, claims that do not answer "why should I care?"

### Sweep 4: Prove It
Is every claim substantiated? Check for unearned superlatives, vague social proof, missing attribution. Common gaps: "Trusted by thousands" (which thousands?), "Industry-leading" (according to whom?).

### Sweep 5: Specificity
Is the copy concrete enough to be compelling? Replace vague language with numbers, timeframes, and examples. "Save time" → "Save 4 hours every week."

### Sweep 6: Heightened Emotion
Does the copy make the reader feel something? Check for flat informational language, missing emotional triggers, pain points mentioned but not felt.

### Sweep 7: Zero Risk
Have we removed every barrier to action? Check friction near CTAs, unanswered objections, missing trust signals, unclear next steps.

## Quality Standards

### Word-Level Checks
Cut: very, really, extremely, just, actually, basically, in order to, things. Replace: utilize→use, implement→set up, leverage→use, facilitate→help, innovative→new, robust→strong, seamless→smooth. Watch for: unnecessary adverbs, passive voice, nominalizations.

### Sentence-Level Checks
One idea per sentence, vary length, front-load important information, max 25 words usually.

### Paragraph-Level Checks
One topic per paragraph, short paragraphs (2–4 sentences for web), strong openings, logical flow, white space for scannability.

## Validation

Complete when: all seven sweeps have been run, no new issues found on final pass, core message preserved through all edits, formatting consistent.

## Restrictions

- Do not change the core message — focus on enhancing it.
- Do not try to fix everything in one pass.
- Do not add emotional texture that crosses into manipulation.

## Output Requirements

- Findings organized by sweep with specific edits recommended.
- Before/after for each significant change.
- Core message preservation confirmed.
