# Marketing Writing

## Purpose
Write to persuade, position, and convert.  
The goal is not merely to sound good. The goal is to move a specific audience toward a specific action.

## Use This Skill For
- landing pages
- ads
- performance copy
- product descriptions
- email sequences
- slogans
- CTA blocks
- campaign messaging
- launch copy
- social conversion copy

## First Decide
Before drafting, identify:
1. audience
2. pain point or desire
3. offer
4. proof or credibility
5. action to drive
6. channel and length constraint

If those are unclear, make the copy conservative and modular.

## Core Principles

### Value Before Flourish
- Lead with the benefit, shift, outcome, or relief.
- Make the reader understand why this matters now.
- Show practical value early.

### Friction Reduction
- Remove ambiguity.
- Remove unnecessary words.
- Make the next step feel easy and obvious.

### Specificity Sells
- Prefer concrete outcomes over grand claims.
- Use numbers, timing, and clear mechanics when available.
- Replace “best-in-class” fluff with evidence, contrast, or clarity.

### Action Orientation
- Every asset should point toward an action:
  - buy
  - book
  - reply
  - start
  - download
  - compare
  - join
  - request demo

## Tone Controls

### Short-Form Performance Copy
- tight
- punchy
- benefit-led
- active voice
- fast rhythm

### Brand Copy
- clear
- memorable
- emotionally coherent
- not overstuffed with slogans

### Premium / B2B Copy
- calm confidence
- commercial seriousness
- less hype, more trust
- proof-led framing

## Suggested Structures

### Landing Page Hero
1. headline
2. subheadline
3. proof / differentiator
4. CTA

### Sales Email
1. hook
2. pain / opportunity
3. offer
4. proof
5. CTA

### Ad Copy
1. problem or desire
2. offer
3. differentiator
4. CTA

## Phrasing Preferences
Use:
- “Get…”
- “See how…”
- “Built for…”
- “Cut…”
- “Launch…”
- “Turn… into…”
- “Book a demo”
- “Start free”
- “See it in action”

## Avoid
- vague superlatives
- fake urgency unless the deadline is real
- generic inspiration language
- cluttered CTA stacks
- too many benefits in one sentence
- passive, apologetic wording
- promotional claims that cannot be supported

## Final Check
Ask internally:
- What exact action is this copy trying to cause?
- Is the value clear in the first lines?
- Is the CTA sharp?
- Would a real buyer understand why this is better or easier?
