## Purpose

Access and apply Anthropic's official brand identity and style resources. Covers brand colors, typography, visual formatting, and styling for branded deliverables.

## When to Use

- When creating Anthropic-branded materials or deliverables
- When applying official brand colors and typography to documents, presentations, or interfaces
- When formatting visual output with Anthropic's corporate identity
- When post-processing or styling deliverables with Anthropic brand assets

## Workflow

1. Review design requirements and target platform.
2. Apply Anthropic brand colors and typography.
3. Use Poppins for headings and Lora for body text (with system fallbacks).
4. Validate visual output against brand standards.
5. Deliver branded assets.

## Quality Standards

- Follow Anthropic's official color palette exactly.
- Use the specified typography hierarchy (Poppins headings, Lora body).
- Maintain consistent spacing and visual rhythm.
- Validate across target formats (PDF, web, slides).

## Technical Rules

Font management:
- Uses system-installed Poppins and Lora fonts when available.
- Automatic fallback to Arial (headings) and Georgia (body).
- No font installation required — works with existing system fonts.
- For best results, pre-install Poppins and Lora fonts in the environment.

## Validation

- Verify brand colors match the official palette.
- Confirm typography hierarchy is applied correctly.
- Check visual consistency across all pages or screens.

## Restrictions

- Do not modify the official brand color palette.
- Do not substitute fonts outside the approved fallback chain.
- Do not apply brand identity to non-Anthropic materials.

## Output Requirements

- Branded deliverables using official colors and typography.
- Consistent application across all pages or sections.
