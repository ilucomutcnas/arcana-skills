## Purpose

Access and apply Anthropic's community brand identity and style resources. Covers community-specific colors, typography, and visual formatting for community-facing materials, events, and communications.

## When to Use

- When creating community-facing Anthropic materials
- When applying community brand colors and typography
- When formatting community deliverables, event materials, or communications
- When distinguishing community materials from official corporate brand

## Workflow

1. Review design requirements and target platform.
2. Apply community brand colors and typography.
3. Use Poppins for headings and Lora for body text (with system fallbacks).
4. Validate visual output against community brand standards.
5. Deliver community-branded assets.

## Quality Standards

- Follow community brand color palette exactly.
- Use the specified typography hierarchy.
- Maintain visual distinction from official corporate materials where required.
- Validate across target formats.

## Technical Rules

Font management:
- Uses system-installed Poppins and Lora fonts when available.
- Automatic fallback to Arial (headings) and Georgia (body).
- No font installation required.

## Validation

- Verify community brand colors match the specified palette.
- Confirm typography hierarchy is applied correctly.
- Check visual consistency across all deliverables.

## Restrictions

- Do not use official corporate brand styling for community materials when community-specific guidelines exist.
- Do not modify the community brand color palette.

## Output Requirements

- Community-branded deliverables using specified colors and typography.
- Consistent application across all pages or sections.
